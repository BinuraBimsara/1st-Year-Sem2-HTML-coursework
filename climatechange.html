WHAT IS CLIMATE CHANGE?

1. Definition

Climate change refers to long-term changes in Earth's temperature and weather patterns. While these changes can happen naturally, modern climate change is mostly driven by human activities.

2. The Science Behind It

Human activities release greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, such as:

  • Carbon dioxide (CO₂)
  • Methane (CH₄)
  • Nitrous oxide (N₂O)

These gases trap heat, causing the planet to warm — a process known as the greenhouse effect.

3. How Fast Is It Happening?

  • Earth has warmed by about 1.1°C since the late 1800s.
  • Most of this warming happened in the last 50 years.
  • Recent years are the hottest ever recorded.

4. Effects of Climate Change

Environment:

  • Melting glaciers and polar ice
  • Rising sea levels
  • More wildfires and floods

People:

  • Heatwaves and extreme weather
  • Water and food shortages
  • Health issues and climate migration

5. What Can We Do?

Mitigation:

  • Use renewable energy (solar, wind)
  • Drive less or use electric cars
  • Plant trees
  • Reduce meat consumption

Adaptation:

  • Prepare for floods and heat
  • Grow climate-resilient crops
  • Design sustainable cities

6. Why It Matters

Climate change affects all life on Earth. Acting now helps protect our health, homes, and future generations. It’s a shared responsibility to care for our planet.
